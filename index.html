The product problem

Kinji started with a simple observation: people often have enough food at home, but still struggle to decide what to cook, what to use first, and what not to buy again.

User pain

Ingredients expire before users notice them. Meal decisions feel harder than they should, especially for busy households.

Behavioural gap

Most users do not maintain a reliable mental model of what they already own, creating overbuying and decision fatigue.

Business opportunity

If Kinji can make savings visible, the product can convert utility into repeat engagement and subscription value.

Data model and instrumentation

The portfolio focuses on measurement readiness: how product behaviour would be captured, analyzed, and turned into roadmap decisions.

Object / Event What it captures Why it matters
User Household size, goals, dietary needs, onboarding path, subscription state. Enables segmentation, personalization, and monetization analysis.
Inventory Item Item name, category, quantity, storage type, purchase date, estimated expiry. Core input for expiry risk, recipe logic, and savings calculation.
Recipe Generated meal, ingredients used, substitutions, dietary constraints, cuisine direction. Connects AI output to engagement and ingredient usage.
Pantry Upload User completed scan or manual inventory setup. Primary activation event and first major onboarding bottleneck.
Ingredient Used Inventory item moved from available to consumed. Measures actual value creation, not just product activity.
Ingredient Expired Item was not used before estimated expiry date. Negative value signal used to improve alerts and recommendations.

KPI framework

The measurement strategy connects user behaviour to business value: activation, engagement, retention, and financial impact.

Activation

  • Pantry setup completion rate
  • Time to first meal recommendation
  • First-session item capture volume

Engagement

  • Recipes generated per active user
  • Use-soon list interactions
  • Inventory updates per week

Retention

  • Week 1 and Week 4 active usage
  • Repeat recipe generation
  • Household routine adoption

Value

  • Estimated savings per household
  • Waste-risk items used before expiry
  • Subscription conversion by savings visibility

Key product insight: the main risk was not AI quality. It was whether users reached value quickly enough.

The product direction shifted toward reducing onboarding friction and creating an instant value loop: capture food → generate a meal → show savings.

Product decisions

These decisions translate discovery into roadmap choices that improve conversion, usability, and measurable value.

Decision 01
Simplify onboarding

Prioritize minimum viable pantry setup over exhaustive inventory capture to reduce abandonment.

Decision 02
Lead with “Tonight’s Meal”

Move the user faster from setup to payoff by showing a practical meal recommendation immediately.

Decision 03
Make savings visible

Connect reduced waste and grocery optimization to a simple ROI story that supports premium conversion.

Experimentation plan

The next phase would validate whether lower-friction onboarding improves activation and repeat usage.

Experiment Hypothesis Primary metric Expected learning
Guided onboarding vs. instant meal Showing a meal earlier increases setup completion. Activation rate Whether users need education or immediate value first.
Savings shown weekly vs. after every meal Frequent savings feedback increases perceived value. Week 2 retention Best cadence for financial reinforcement.
Manual input vs. receipt-first flow Passive capture reduces onboarding effort. Time to inventory setup Best ingestion strategy for scale.

Monetization logic

The subscription strategy is anchored to visible user value. The more clearly Kinji shows savings, the easier it becomes to justify a paid tier.

Free utility

Basic pantry tracking, limited meal suggestions, and simple expiry reminders.

Premium value

Advanced AI planning, deeper personalization, grocery savings insights, and household collaboration.

Business model

Annual subscription incentive, future grocery partnerships, and price-aware recommendation loops.
